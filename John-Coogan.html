💡 Ideas & Insights (Overview) ▼

John Coogan's key learnings, business philosophy, and insights from building Soylent, Lucy, and his YouTube channel. These ideas shape his approach to entrepreneurship and content creation.

Peter Thiel's Advice▼
Context: Peter Thiel's feedback about Soylent's business model and why it should have been run as a lifestyle business, not a venture startup.
"We went in for the pitch and sat down [with Peter Thiel] And Peter was just kind of like, 'Yeah, this is just a widgets business. Run this like any other business. You need to sell. You have product–market fit, you just need to maximize. Run the playbook: bring the customer in, make sure you're bringing them in cheaper.' He was basically like, 'This is a great lifestyle business, guys. Congrats.' And then we raised a bunch of venture, and it was a disaster"
— John (Sep 2023)
Soylent → Lucy Learnings▼
Context: How learning from Soylent's mistakes and keeping the core team helped Lucy focus on quality and adapt quickly.
"It's very very hard to start one of these companies and I couldn't have done it unless we had the first company under our belt"
— John, Terra Api, 7/23
"We were definitely sharper on the manufacturing side—made fewer mistakes [with Lucy]. Made fewer mistakes in a lot of different ways. Just had a much, much higher focus on quality, on keeping things in stock, and on doing best practices from day one"
— John (Jun 2023)
"We kept a lot of the same team. I think 4 of the core people at Soylent came over to Lucy, and that was basically our first 4 people. As we grew, we were definitely hiring for flexibility, because we knew there would be a pivot point where we'd move from DTC to retail. Having the ability to actually change how the business works over time and be flexible in that regard was important. We were also focused on people who were really, really good at leveraging existing tools. So we really tried to lean into the existing platforms and keep the company lean. Even today, I think we're still under 10 people. It's pretty small"
— John (Jun 2023)
Lucy's Founding Story▼
Context: The motivation and story behind the launch of Lucy, centered around nicotine harm reduction and founder experience.
Nicotine is the future
"David Renteln was smoking kind of casually. [...] So he started looking at the problem from a more scientific perspective"
— John (Jul 2020)
"We started a second company, called Lucy, with the goal of helping people quit smoking. One of my co-founders was quitting smoking, and a lot of our friends were vaping. We knew that there were going to be people who wanted to quit. Nicorette wasn't really doing the job for a lot of people—they kind of saw it as more of a medicine. So we thought we could create a new nicotine gum, new nicotine products, to try and help people get off of the hardcore stuff like cigarettes."
— John (Jul 2023)
Lucy Marketing Strategy▼
Context: The deliberate marketing focus to avoid Juul's mistakes and ensure the brand's targeting was responsible.
"We kind of shied away from going broad and viral with our marketing because Juul had already gone viral in the tobacco and nicotine space, and they wound up attracting a bunch of kids, which was very detrimental to their business in the long term. We didn't want to get kids to use this. We really wanted to focus on that older millennial consumer. So we were much more targeted, on Facebook, Instagram, and podcasts, really trying to get it into the right people's hands"
— John (Jun 2023)

"I'm one day a week at Lucy, mostly helping out with stuff like doing content and then, ideally, brokering deals with other content creators"
— John (Sep 2023)
@JohnCooganPlus YouTube Origin▼
Context: How John started on YouTube, the Clubhouse context, and his early experiments.
"In early 2020, I couldn't get an invite to Clubhouse. All the cool kids were hanging out there. So I made a YouTube video analyzing Clubhouse [on his old channel, Termsheets]. Everyone was saying it was massively overvalued, and I was kind of like, 'Hey, it doesn't matter. They're taking a big risk, they're taking a big shot. At $100M, yeah, it probably wound up being overvalued—but at the very least, just let them go and experiment.'"
— John (Sep 2023)
"During COVID, I was just bored and had free time for the first time in like a decade of entrepreneurship. But it was very clear that it was a time where, if you got louder online, you could actually make friends and meet new people. I just kind of thought it would be interesting to do the traditional take on 'Here's an opinion about technology' or 'Here's the history of a company,' but with more of a YouTube-native format. There were a couple of channels that would do something like 'The history of Apple,' but it was very much from an outsider's perspective"
— John (Nov 2024)
First Year of YouTube (The Grind)▼
Context: The grind, the lack of traction, and what John learned about process and community.
"The first year was really slow. I think it took almost a full year to get more than a hundred views on a video, like 50 episodes in a row. But I didn't care. I didn't know what success looked like, because at the time, I was the only person in tech doing this. Garry Tan was around, but he was already huge. I wasn't even comparing myself to him. When I finally broke 100 views on a video, I was fired up. I was like, 'This one's a banger!' I just loved the process. I was learning motion graphics, spending hours in After Effects"
— John (Apr 2025)
First Viral Videos (Breakthrough)▼
Context: Which topics and content finally went viral, and why.
Title: The Only Reason SpaceX Works (Gwynne Shotwell)
"The very first one was about SpaceX, and it was about a topic that a VC had told me years ago, that everyone gives Elon all the credit, and obviously he deserves a ton of credit for SpaceX… So it hit a few different things. First off, everyone likes looking at rockets, and Elon is also a household name. It's much harder to make a viral video about some small SAAS company that only people in tech know about… That video blew up and got around 60,000 views, which was really big"
— John (Nov 2024)
Title: You Don't Know the Metaverse...
"Then later, when the metaverse stuff happened, those videos started getting millions and millions of views. That was the first time it went really, really big, because it was something that the mainstream media was talking about. Because I was in tech, I'd been talking about it with everyone months before. People in the space kind of knew there was going to be this VR resurgence. There was a discussion about it in tech, but it took a while until Zuck rebranded to Meta. That was the moment when everyone needed to talk about it. Just being early there, with some really solid content. By then I had really figured out the format, really figured out the thumbnails and all that stuff — the video just went mega viral"
— John (Nov 2024)
Benefits of Running a Channel▼
Context: How making YouTube content led to connections with entrepreneurs, hiring, and storytelling skills.
"A great way to meet an entrepreneur is make a video about their company."
— John, Media Empires, 09/23
"[My channel] has helped with hiring. It's just great. Obviously, you're putting content out to a lot of people—it's very easy to pull from that audience to hire someone if you need to. And then, on a bigger scale, it's just helped with storytelling"
— John (Feb 2023)
💼 Business Philosophy & Future▼
Building for the Long Term▼
"The biggest lesson from Soylent is that sometimes the right path isn't the obvious venture path. There's real value in building profitable, sustainable businesses that don't need to grow at all costs"
— John (Sep 2023)
Founders Fund Role▼
"Being at Founders Fund has given me a unique perspective on what makes companies successful at scale. It's not just about the initial idea - it's about execution, timing, and having the right team to navigate challenges"
— John (Jul 2024)
Content Creation Philosophy▼
"Consistency beats perfection every time. I'd rather put out a good video every week than spend months on one perfect video. The audience appreciates the regular value, and you learn faster by shipping more frequently"
— John (Sep 2023)
